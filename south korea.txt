    <h2 style="font-family: verdana">About South Korea;</h2>
    <blockquote style="font-family: courier">
      South Korea, officially known as the Republic of Korea (ROK), is a country
      in East Asia, located on the southern half of the Korean Peninsula. It
      shares a border with North Korea to the north and is surrounded by the
      Yellow Sea and the Sea of Japan (East Sea). The capital and largest city
      is Seoul, a global technology and cultural hub. South Korea has a
      population of about 52 million people and operates as a democratic
      republic with a president as its head of state. The country has
      experienced rapid economic growth since the mid-20th century, transforming
      into one of the world's leading economies, known for its industries in
      technology, automobiles, and entertainment. Major global companies like
      Samsung, Hyundai, and LG originate from South Korea. The country is famous
      for its rich culture and history, including ancient palaces, Buddhist
      temples, and traditional hanbok clothing. It is also a global leader in
      pop culture, with K-pop music, Korean dramas (K-dramas), and Korean
      cuisine gaining international popularity. Dishes like kimchi, bibimbap,
      and Korean BBQ are widely loved. South Korea has a highly developed
      transportation and technology sector, with high-speed internet, bullet
      trains (KTX), and advanced robotics. It is also known for hosting major
      international events, such as the 2018 Winter Olympics.
